Cat® Energy Control System (ECS) 200 – Documentation Suite

The Cat® ECS 200 is a paralleling generator set controller designed for multi-unit power systems in island and utility paralleling applications. Built on a single, versatile hardware platform, it supports a broad range of genset control scenarios — from standalone emergency power to complex multi-generator paralleling switchgear with automatic load management. The system is configured and monitored through a dedicated touchscreen HMI, the Cat Electronic Technician (ET) service tool, and remote SCADA (Modbus TCP/IP) connections.

This documentation suite comprises 5 specification manuals totaling 979 pages, covering the controller hardware, HMI operator interface, core paralleling genset software, Balance of Plant (BOP) ancillary controls for CHP/container applications, and the SCADA Modbus data interface. Together they define the complete system from physical installation through software configuration to remote supervisory integration.

🔧 Controller Hardware Manual

Defines the physical hardware platform shared by both the ECS 100 and ECS 200 product lines. The controller is a single-board module whose functionality is determined entirely by the loaded application software. This manual focuses exclusively on the electrical and mechanical specifications — software behaviour is covered in separate documents.

  • 4 × 4-wire Ethernet ports (10/100 Mbps)
  • 2 × 2-wire Ethernet ports (SPE / 10BASE-T1L)
  • 1 × Primary CAN bus port
  • 1 × Accessory CAN bus port
  • 1 × RS-485 serial port
Signal Inputs
  • 12 × Discrete digital inputs
  • 1 × Isolated digital input
  • 3 × Analog inputs (resistive or voltage mode, shared 5V supply)
  • 1 × MPU (Magnetic Pick-Up) speed sensing input
  • 2 × 3-phase AC voltage sensing inputs (generator + bus/utility)
  • 1 × 3-phase AC current sensing input (via CTs)
Signal Outputs
  • 12 × Sinking digital outputs
  • 2 × Analog outputs (voltage or current mode)
  • 3 × PWM outputs

The manual covers mounting/installation procedures with DIN rail and panel-mount options, grounding requirements, wire gauge recommendations (14–20 AWG depending on signal type, shielded twisted pair for communications), power supply requirements, LED indicator meanings (power/firmware status, CAN 1/2 communication), AC metering accuracy specifications, and the programming/firmware update interface.

🏭 Balance of Plant (BOP) Software Operations Manual

Covers the auxiliary plant control features that extend the ECS 200 beyond core genset control into Combined Heat and Power (CHP) and containerized power plant applications. The BOP software manages thermal circuits — cooling water, aftercooler, customer heating, exhaust heat recovery — as well as enclosure ventilation and safety systems. Each feature section follows a consistent structure: functional description, control logic flow diagrams, setpoints, diagnostics/status events, I/O mapping, and HMI display requirements.

Cooling Circuit Controls
  • SCAC 3-Way Valve & Pump (§5) – Separate Circuit Aftercooler with PID-controlled 3-way mixing valve, end position sensors, pump follow-up delay timer, immediate stop logic
  • SCAC Radiator (§6) – Aftercooler radiator with VFD (variable frequency drive) or staged single-speed fan switching, temperature setpoint calculation
  • Engine Cooling Valve & Pump (§7) – Jacket water 3-way valve control with pump management
  • Engine Cooling Radiator (§8) – Engine circuit radiator with VFD or staged fan, PID control, temperature setpoint logic
  • Dual Core Radiator (§9) – Combined SCAC + JW radiator control for power-only mode with VFD or stage switching, control deviation calculation
Heat Recovery & CHP
  • Customer Site Heating (§10) – Heating circuit with cooling water heat exchanger, 3-way valve (can be installed in heating circuit or engine cooling circuit), pump control
  • Exhaust Heat Exchanger Bypass (§11) – EHE bypass control for customer heating with exhaust gas valve management, multiple operational scenarios with flow diagrams
  • Exhaust Gas Temp Sensors (§14) – Additional exhaust temperature I/O for CHP monitoring, trip setpoint calculations, and auxiliary temperature sensor configuration
Ventilation & Safety
  • Enclosure Ventilation (§12) – Container/enclosure ventilation with and without circulating air, ventilation fan control, inlet/outlet louver control, standby ventilation mode
  • Smoke/Fire Alarm (§13) – Fire alarm reaction (ventilation shutdown), gas alarm reaction (full ventilation), prioritization logic between fire and gas alarm scenarios
BOP I/O & Integration
  • BOP Ethernet I/O Module (§15) – Remote I/O module configuration for BOP signals
  • Config Code: CHP & Container – I/O mapping for CHP + container applications
  • Config Code: JW & SCAC – I/O mapping for jacket water + aftercooler
  • Config Code: Combined – Full JW, SCAC, CHP, and container I/O mapping
  • HMI display requirements for all BOP DI/DO/AI/AO points

Every BOP feature includes comprehensive control setpoint tables (enable/disable, temperature thresholds, PID gains, timers, hysteresis), diagnostic event definitions (sensor failures, actuator faults, communication errors), status events, data feedback parameters for monitoring, complete I/O allocation tables, and HMI screen layouts showing overview and configuration screens with parameter labels and value formats.

  • Byte Packing – Combining multiple command values into a single write register
  • State/Status/Command – Enumerated state values, status bit flags, command codes
  • Complex Data – Multi-register structured data for event logs and timestamped records
Genset & Engine Register Maps
  • Genset Ratings – Active group, rated voltage, current, power, frequency

  • Load Share / LSLD – Load demand status, kW setpoint, add/drop commands
  • Paralleling Data – Gen #1 through Gen #32 paralleling status registers
  • Engine Data – Service meter, speed, fuel rate, oil pressure/temp, coolant temp, exhaust temps, intake, turbocharger, filter differential, aftertreatment (DEF level, DPF soot, SCR temp), gas data, dynamic gas blending

  • Acknowledge/Reset – Remote event acknowledgement and alarm reset commands
  • Alarm Bit Packing – Hard shutdown, soft shutdown, lamp, horn, display events packed into user-configurable alarm groups
  • Annunciator – Remote annunciator status registers

  • Network Status – Communication health for all connected modules

  • Programmable Cycle Timer & Real-Time Clock – Timer status and RTC read/write

⚡ Best Practices from SCADA Manual §13
  • Batch reads — Read contiguous register blocks in a single FC03 transaction (up to 125 registers). The groups below are arranged to support this.
  • Data prioritization — Poll critical registers every 1 s, slow-changing data every 5–30 s. Use the User Alarm Groups (regs 101–110) for event-driven alerting instead of polling hundreds of event registers.
  • Persistent connections — Keep TCP connections open; do not create a new socket per transaction.
  • Retry with limits — Implement retries for failed reads but limit to 2–3 attempts. Use timeouts of 1000–3000 ms.
  • Two-word registers — Power/energy registers are 32-bit (2 words). MSW is at the lower address. Combine as: value = (MSW << 16) | LSW, then apply scaling and offset.

📐 Scaling Quick Reference
  • Voltage: raw × 1 V/bit = Volts
  • Current: raw × 1 A/bit = Amps
  • Frequency: raw × (1/128) = Hz
  • Power Factor: raw × (1/16384) – 2 = PF
  • Real Power (2-word): ((MSW≪16)|LSW) × 1 W – 2,000,000,000 = Watts
  • Reactive Power (2-word): same as real power but VAR
  • Apparent Power (2-word): ((MSW≪16)|LSW) × 1 VA = VA
  • Energy (2-word): ((MSW≪16)|LSW) × 1 Wh = Wh
  • Oil Pressure: raw × (1/128) = PSI
  • Temperature: raw × (1/32) – 273 = °F
  • Battery Voltage: raw × (1/20) = Volts
  • Engine Speed: raw × 0.125 = RPM
  • Engine Hours (2-word): ((MSW≪16)|LSW) × 0.05 = Hours
  • Percent Load: raw × 0.4 = %
  • Fuel Level: raw × (1/2.56) = %
